# Broadcasting on Beacon

Welcome to the **Broadcasting Guide** for Beacon! This guide explains how to start your stream, configure your settings, and use the powerful features within the built-in Broadcast Studio to engage your audience.

Beacon uses WebRTC for low-latency, peer-to-peer (P2P) streaming directly from your browser. There is no need for external software like OBS or Streamlabs to get started.

## Accessing the Broadcast Studio

To start broadcasting, simply log in to your Beacon account and navigate to the **Broadcast** tab from the main navigation menu. The Broadcast Studio provides everything you need to manage your stream and interact with your community.

## Setting Up Your Stream

Before going live, take a moment to set up your stream details:

### 1. Stream Information
In the **Stream Info** section, you can set the metadata for your stream:
-   **Title:** Enter a catchy title that tells viewers what you're doing.
-   **Tags:** Add relevant tags separated by commas (e.g., `Gaming, Just Chatting, Dev`) to help users discover your channel.
-   **Go Live Notification:** (Optional) Set a custom message that your followers will receive when you go live.
-   **Update Info:** Click the `Update Info` button to save your changes. You can update this information at any time, even while live.

### 2. Camera, Microphone, and Screen Sharing
The main video player in the Broadcast Studio shows a preview of your stream. Hover over the video player to access the controls:
-   **Camera:** Click the camera icon to toggle your webcam on or off.
-   **Microphone:** Click the microphone icon to mute or unmute your audio.
-   **Screen Share:** Click the monitor icon to share your screen. You can choose to share your entire screen, a specific window, or a browser tab.

## Going Live

Once your stream info is set and your camera/microphone are ready, you're ready to start!

1.  Click the **Go Live** button in the top right corner.
2.  Your stream will instantly be broadcasted to the Beacon network using the P2P mesh.
3.  The top bar will display your live viewer count (Connected Peers) and a **Stream Health Indicator**, which monitors the quality of your connection to the mesh.

To end your stream, simply click the red **End Stream** button.

## Engaging Your Audience

The Broadcast Studio includes several tools to help you engage with your viewers:

### Live Chat
When you are live, the **Stream Chat** panel on the right side of the screen becomes active. Here you can see messages from your viewers and participate in the conversation in real-time.

### Polls
Want to ask your audience a question? Use the built-in polling feature!
1.  Under **Quick Actions**, click **Start Poll**.
2.  Enter your question and up to 5 options.
3.  Click **Create Poll**. The poll will instantly appear in the chat for your viewers to vote on.
4.  You can end an active poll early by clicking the **End Active Poll** button under Quick Actions.

### Co-Streaming and Revenue Splits
Beacon makes collaboration easy and rewarding with **Co-Streaming Revenue Splits**. If you are streaming with friends or guests, you can automatically split any tips and revenue generated during the stream.
1.  In the **Co-Streaming Panel**, click **Add Member**.
2.  Enter the username of the guest and the percentage of the revenue they should receive.
3.  Ensure the total split equals exactly 100%.
4.  Click **Update Squad**. The Beacon backend will now securely distribute all tips to the squad members according to the set percentages.

### Triggering Ad Breaks
If your channel is eligible for ad revenue, you can trigger a manual ad break for your viewers. Under **Quick Actions**, click the **Ad Break** button.
*(Note: Viewers acting as relay nodes also earn credits when ads are played, incentivizing them to help deliver your content!)*

## Troubleshooting Stream Issues

-   **Camera/Microphone Not Working:** Ensure that your browser has permission to access your camera and microphone. You may need to check your browser's site settings.
-   **Poor Stream Health:** If your Stream Health Indicator shows a poor connection, check your internet upload speed. Beacon requires a stable connection to reliably relay your stream through the mesh network. Ensure no other applications are heavily using your upload bandwidth.

For more detailed technical support, see the [Troubleshooting Guide](troubleshooting.md).
